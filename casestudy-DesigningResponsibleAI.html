Designing Responsible AI

Building Inclusivity in AI-Powered Experiences

Presentation Overview

Casting a Vision of how Design Thinkers and Practitioners can advocate for fair and inclusive outcomes in the design of AI-powered experiences.

Before an audience of design colleagues at Capital One, I made the case as to why a Designer's ability to anticipate AI's impact on human outcomes play a pivotal role in analyzing the suitability of data variables for machine learning algorithms. Particularly when it comes to mitigating social bias and elevating customer trust—in order to improve business outcomes and reputation.

The presentation is outlined in 4 parts:

1: When AI is Designed to Exclude

Exclusion happens by design. This holds true for three real-life tales where 1 or 2 decisions in the algorithm design led to the exclusion of people groups on a massive scale. For each tales, we will identify the ethical stumbling point, and what could have been done to reduce or eliminate it

2: Advocating for Inclusion

Practical ways that Design Thinkers and Practitioners can meaningfully contribute to buidling Responsible AI.

3: It Takes Courage

A personal story of when I identified an ethical risk, and gathered the courage to speak up about it.

4: When AI is Designed to Include

When AI widens the opportunity gate and generates business value.
